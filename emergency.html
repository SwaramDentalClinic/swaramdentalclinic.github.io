What Qualifies

What Is a Dental Emergency?

A dental emergency is any situation where you have severe pain, visible damage, risk of infection, or tooth loss that requires immediate attention. Delaying treatment can make the condition worse, more painful, and more expensive to treat.

At Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi, we prioritise emergency patients. If you call us, we will do our best to see you the same day during clinic hours.

We Treat

Common Dental Emergencies We Handle

From severe toothache to knocked-out teeth, our team provides immediate relief and expert treatment.

🬔

Severe Toothache

Intense, throbbing or constant tooth pain usually indicates pulp infection or abscess. We diagnose the cause and provide immediate pain relief — often with emergency root canal treatment.

🥷

Broken or Chipped Tooth

A broken tooth can be sharp, painful, and expose inner layers to infection. We smooth, bond, or crown the tooth to restore function and appearance in the same visit where possible.

🚨

Knocked-Out Tooth

Time is critical — a knocked-out tooth can often be re-implanted if you reach us within 30–60 minutes. Keep the tooth moist in milk or saline and call immediately.

🩸

Dental Abscess

A dental abscess is a bacterial infection with swelling, fever, and severe pain. It can be dangerous if it spreads. We drain the abscess, prescribe antibiotics, and treat the underlying cause.

💊

Lost Crown or Filling

A dislodged crown or filling can leave your tooth sensitive and exposed to damage. We re-cement existing crowns or place a new restoration to protect your tooth the same day.

🔴

Gum Bleeding & Swelling

Uncontrolled gum bleeding or sudden severe gum swelling may indicate infection or trauma. We provide immediate assessment and treatment to stop bleeding and resolve the infection.

Before You Reach Us

Dental Emergency First Aid

What to do immediately while you make your way to Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi.

  1. 1

    Knocked-Out Tooth

    Handle by the crown only, never the root. Rinse gently with milk or saline. Try to replace it in the socket, or store in milk. Call us immediately — time is critical. Best results within 30 minutes.

  2. 2

    Severe Toothache

    Take ibuprofen or paracetamol as directed. Apply a cold compress to the cheek. Avoid hot, cold or sweet foods. Do not place aspirin on the tooth. Call us at +91 84848 51686.

  3. 3

    Broken or Cracked Tooth

    Rinse your mouth with warm water. Collect any large pieces. Apply cold compress for swelling. Cover sharp edges with dental wax if available. See us as soon as possible.

  4. 4

    Dental Abscess / Swelling

    Do not attempt to drain the abscess yourself. Rinse with warm salt water. Take prescribed or OTC antibiotics only if already directed by a dentist. Seek care urgently — swelling that affects breathing is a medical emergency.

  5. 5

    Lost Crown or Filling

    Apply dental cement (from a pharmacy) or sugar-free gum as a temporary seal. Keep the crown safe to bring along. Avoid chewing on that side. Call Swaram Dental Clinic to arrange a same-day visit.

  6. 6

    Reach Us — Open 7 Days
