Why Teeth Discolour

Common Causes of Tooth Staining & Discolouration

Understanding why your teeth are discoloured helps determine the right whitening approach. Dr. Swati Shivane assesses stain type before every whitening treatment.

  • Coffee, tea & red wine — the most common cause of extrinsic (surface) staining in Hinjewadi's working population
  • Tobacco & smoking — causes deep yellow-brown staining that builds up over time
  • Natural ageing — enamel thins with age, revealing the yellower dentine layer beneath
  • Certain foods & spices — turmeric, curries, berries, and tomato sauces can stain over time
  • Medications & antibiotics — tetracycline taken during tooth development causes intrinsic (internal) grey-brown staining
  • Fluorosis — excess fluoride during childhood can cause white spots or mottled brown patches on enamel

What to Expect

The Teeth Whitening Process at Swaram Dental Clinic

Professional, supervised, and completed in a single visit. Here's exactly what happens from the moment you arrive.

  1. 01

    Pre-Whitening Assessment

    Dr. Swati Shivane checks your teeth for any existing decay, gum issues, or sensitivity that should be addressed before whitening. Your current shade is recorded on a standardised shade guide so you can compare results objectively after treatment.

  2. 02

    Professional Cleaning

    A professional scale and polish removes surface tartar and plaque before whitening — ensuring the whitening gel contacts clean enamel for maximum effectiveness. This step makes a significant difference to your final result.

  3. 03

    Gum Protection

    A soft gum barrier is carefully applied to protect your gum tissue from the whitening agent. This is a critical step that ensures the treatment is safe and comfortable — and distinguishes professional whitening from DIY alternatives.

  4. 04

    Whitening Gel Application

    Professional-grade whitening gel is applied evenly across all visible teeth. The gel is activated and left for a set time — this cycle is repeated 2–3 times within the session to achieve maximum whitening depth and coverage.

  5. 05

    Shade Assessment & Reveal

    After treatment, the gel and gum barrier are removed, your teeth are rinsed, and your final shade is compared against your starting shade. Most patients achieve 4–8 shades brighter — the difference is immediately visible and striking.

  6. 06

    Aftercare Instructions

    Dr. Swati Shivane provides personalised aftercare guidance — what to eat and avoid for the first 48 hours, how to maintain your results, and when to return for a touch-up session to keep your smile at its brightest.
