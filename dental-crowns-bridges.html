What Are Dental Crowns & Bridges?

Restore, Protect & Replace with Precision Crowns & Bridges

A dental crown (also called a dental cap) is a custom-made tooth-shaped restoration that fits over a damaged, decayed, or weakened tooth — covering it entirely from the gum line upward. It restores the tooth's shape, size, strength, and appearance while protecting it from further damage.

A dental bridge replaces one or more missing teeth. It is anchored to the natural teeth (or implants) on either side of the gap, with an artificial tooth (pontic) suspended in between. Unlike dentures, a bridge is permanently fixed — you eat, speak, and smile with complete confidence.

At Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi, Dr. Swati Shivane — MDS postgraduate specialist in Endodontics & Conservative Dentistry — personally designs and places every crown and bridge. Using zirconia, full-ceramic, and metal-free materials, Dr. Shivane ensures each restoration is indistinguishable from your natural teeth in colour, shape, and translucency.

Why choose a specialist? An MDS in Conservative Dentistry means postgraduate training specifically in tooth restoration and the principles of occlusion (bite). Crown Options

Types of Dental Crowns at Swaram Dental Clinic

Dr. Swati Shivane recommends the right crown material based on the tooth location, bite forces, and your aesthetic goals. All premium options are metal-free.

👑

Zirconia Crown

The strongest, most durable metal-free crown. Ideal for back molars and front teeth. Highly aesthetic, biocompatible, long-lasting. Our most popular choice.

💎

Full Ceramic (E.max / Porcelain)

Maximum aesthetic translucency — virtually indistinguishable from natural enamel. Best for front teeth where natural appearance is the top priority.

🔨

PFM (Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal)

A metal substructure with porcelain outer layer. Durable and affordable but may show a grey line at the gum margin over time. Less preferred over zirconia now.

⚡

Implant Crown

A zirconia or ceramic crown placed on a dental implant post to replace a missing tooth completely and permanently.

Bridge Options

Types of Dental Bridges

A dental bridge is the fastest, non-surgical way to replace a missing tooth. Dr. Swati Shivane will advise the best bridge type for your specific case.

  • Traditional Bridge — anchored to crowned natural teeth on both sides. Most common type.
  • Cantilever Bridge — anchored to one tooth only. Used where only one adjacent tooth is available.
  • Maryland Bridge — metal or ceramic wings bonded to backs of adjacent teeth. Minimal tooth preparation required.
  • Implant-Supported Bridge — anchored to dental implants for maximum stability and longevity.
Do You Need a Crown or Bridge?

Common Reasons for Dental Crowns & Bridges in Hinjewadi

If you have any of the following conditions, Dr. Swati Shivane may recommend a crown or bridge at your consultation at Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi.

After Root Canal Treatment

A crown is strongly recommended after RCT to protect the treated, now-brittle tooth from fracture. Most RCT-treated teeth should be crowned as soon as possible.

Severely Decayed Tooth

When decay is too extensive for a filling to restore the tooth adequately, a crown covers and protects the remaining tooth structure.

Cracked or Fractured Tooth

A crown holds a cracked tooth together, preventing the crack from propagating and causing pain or tooth loss.

Worn Down Teeth

Teeth worn by grinding (bruxism) or acid erosion can be restored to full height and function with crowns.

Missing Tooth (Bridge)

A dental bridge replaces a missing tooth without surgery, restoring your smile, bite function, and preventing adjacent teeth from drifting.

Cosmetic Improvement

Crowns can improve the appearance of severely discoloured, misshapen, or heavily restored teeth as part of a smile makeover.

Step by Step

How Dental Crowns & Bridges Are Done at Swaram Dental Clinic

Dr. Swati Shivane follows a meticulous, evidence-based protocol to ensure every crown and bridge fits precisely, looks natural, and lasts for years.

  1. 01

    Examination & X-ray Assessment

    Dr. Swati Shivane examines the tooth, assesses bite, takes a digital X-ray to check root health, and discusses crown or bridge options with you.

  2. 02

    Tooth Preparation Under Local Anaesthesia

    The tooth is shaped to receive the crown — a small amount of enamel is removed from all sides. This is done completely painlessly under local anaesthesia.

  3. 03

    Impression & Shade Selection

    A precise impression of the prepared tooth and surrounding teeth is taken. Shade is carefully matched to your adjacent natural teeth under proper lighting for a seamless result.

  4. 04

    Temporary Crown Placement

    A temporary crown protects the prepared tooth while the permanent zirconia or ceramic crown is fabricated at our dental lab — typically 5–7 days.

  5. 05

    Crown Fabrication

    Your permanent crown is milled from high-strength zirconia block or hand-layered from ceramic by a skilled dental technician for maximum aesthetic precision.

  6. 06

    Trial Fitting & Bite Adjustment

    Dr. Swati Shivane trial-fits the crown, checks the bite from all angles, and makes any necessary adjustments before final cementation. Your comfort and bite alignment are verified.

  7. 07

    Permanent Cementation

    The crown is permanently bonded with a dental cement. Final bite check, polishing, and aftercare instructions complete your appointment.

After Your Crown or Bridge

Aftercare & How Long Do Crowns Last?

With proper care, a zirconia or ceramic crown placed by Dr. Swati Shivane at Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi, can last 15–20 years or more. Bridges typically last 10–15 years.

What to Expect After Fitting

  • Mild sensitivity for 1–2 weeks after crown placement — normal and temporary
  • The bite may feel slightly different for a few days — this settles as you adapt
  • Temporary crowns: avoid sticky or hard foods until the permanent crown is cemented
  • Call us if the temporary crown comes off — we will re-cement it same day

Long-Term Care for Crowns & Bridges

  • Brush twice daily with a non-abrasive toothpaste
  • Floss daily — use a floss threader or interdental brush under bridges
  • Avoid biting extremely hard objects (ice, hard nuts, bottle caps)
  • Wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth at night
  • 6-monthly check-ups at Swaram Dental Clinic to monitor crown integrity and gum health
Crown vs Alternatives

When Is a Crown Better Than Other Options?

Dr. Swati Shivane always recommends the most conservative treatment possible. Here is how crowns compare to alternatives.

Crown vs Large Filling

When more than 50% of a tooth structure is lost to decay or fracture, a filling alone cannot provide adequate protection. A crown is the correct solution.

Bridge vs Dental Implant

A bridge is faster and non-surgical. A dental implant is the gold standard for tooth replacement with no need to involve adjacent teeth.

Strongly recommended. An RCT-treated tooth without a crown has a significantly higher fracture rate. Crown placement is an essential part of the RCT treatment plan.
