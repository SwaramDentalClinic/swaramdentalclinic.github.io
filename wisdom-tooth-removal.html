What is Wisdom Tooth Removal?

Expert Wisdom Tooth Extraction in Hinjewadi

Wisdom tooth removal (third molar extraction) is the surgical or simple extraction of one or more of the four wisdom teeth — the last molars at the back of your mouth — when they are causing pain, infection, crowding, or damage to neighbouring teeth.

At Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi, Dr. Swati Shivane — MDS specialist — personally conducts your consultation, takes a digital OPG X-ray, assesses the impaction, and plans your treatment. Our experienced MDS-qualified specialist team then carries out the extraction with precision, under local anaesthesia, for a completely painless experience.

Wisdom teeth (third molars) typically erupt between ages 17–25. When there is insufficient space in the jaw, they become impacted — partially or fully trapped — leading to pain, swelling, infection, damage to adjacent teeth, and cyst formation.

Why timely removal matters: Leaving a problematic wisdom tooth untreated leads to progressive bone damage, infection that can spread to surrounding teeth and jaw, and more complex surgery later. If you are searching for wisdom tooth removal near me in Hinjewadi, early intervention at Swaram Dental Clinic ensures simpler, faster, and safer treatment.

Do You Need Removal?

Signs Your Wisdom Tooth Needs to Come Out

If you experience any of these symptoms, visit Swaram Dental Clinic in Hinjewadi immediately. Early treatment prevents serious complications.

  • Persistent pain at the back of the mouth — especially when biting or chewing
  • Swelling of gums or jaw around the wisdom tooth area
  • Infection (pericoronitis) — gum flap over partial eruption traps food and bacteria
  • Crowding of adjacent teeth — wisdom tooth pushing other teeth out of alignment
  • Damage to neighbouring second molar — impacted tooth pressing against it
  • Cyst or abscess — fluid-filled sac forming around an impacted wisdom tooth
Types of Impaction

Understanding Impacted Wisdom Teeth

↗

Mesial Impaction

Tooth angled forward toward the front of the mouth — the most common type of impaction, often requiring surgical extraction.

→

Horizontal Impaction

Tooth lying completely sideways — the most complex impaction, always requiring surgical removal. Causes maximum pressure on the adjacent second molar.

↘

Distal Impaction

Tooth angled toward the back of the mouth — less common but can still cause significant pain and crowding.

↑

Partial Eruption

Tooth partially through the gum — creates a pocket where bacteria accumulate, leading to recurring infection (pericoronitis).

Why Remove It?

Benefits of Wisdom Tooth Removal Over Leaving It

A problematic wisdom tooth will not improve on its own. Timely removal prevents escalating complications and protects your oral health.

Immediate Pain Relief

Removing the offending wisdom tooth eliminates the source of pain, pressure, and throbbing discomfort. Most patients feel significant relief within 24–48 hours of extraction.

Stop Infection in Its Tracks

An infected impacted wisdom tooth can spread to the jaw, throat, and neck if untreated. Timely removal at Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi eliminates the infection source completely.

Protect Adjacent Teeth

An impacted wisdom tooth pressing against the second molar causes damage, decay, and root resorption. Removing it early prevents costly treatment on neighbouring healthy teeth.

Prevent Cysts & Tumours

Impacted wisdom teeth can develop dentigerous cysts that destroy surrounding bone and teeth. Early extraction completely eliminates this risk.

Prevent Teeth Crowding

Wisdom teeth pushing against adjacent teeth can undo years of orthodontic work and cause misalignment. Removal preserves your smile and bite alignment.

Simpler & Safer When Done Early

Wisdom tooth roots are shorter and the surrounding bone is less dense in younger patients. Early removal means faster healing, less complexity, and lower risk of complications.

Step by Step

How Wisdom Tooth Removal is Done at Swaram Dental Clinic

Our evidence-based protocol ensures a painless, efficient, and safe wisdom tooth extraction experience in Hinjewadi.

  1. 01

    Consultation & Digital X-ray / OPG

    Dr. Swati Shivane conducts your consultation and takes a digital X-ray or OPG (panoramic X-ray) to assess the wisdom tooth's position, angulation, root morphology, and proximity to the inferior alveolar nerve.

  2. 02

    Treatment Planning

    Dr. Swati reviews the X-ray findings, determines whether simple or surgical removal is required, explains the full procedure, risks, and aftercare, and assigns the case to our specialist extraction team.

  3. 03

    Local Anaesthesia

    The area is thoroughly numbed with local anaesthetic. Topical gel is applied first to make even the injection comfortable. You feel pressure during the procedure but absolutely no pain.

  4. 04

    Extraction — Simple or Surgical

    For an erupted tooth: simple extraction using dental forceps. For an impacted tooth: a small incision is made in the gum, bone is carefully removed if needed, and the tooth is sectioned if required for safe removal.

  5. 05

    Suturing

    If a surgical incision was made, the area is sutured (stitched) with dissolvable sutures to promote healing and reduce post-operative discomfort.

  6. 06

    Gauze & Bleeding Control

    A gauze pad is placed over the extraction site and you bite down gently for 30–45 minutes to help the blood clot form. This is the most critical step in preventing dry socket.

  7. 07

    Post-Operative Care Instructions

    Detailed written and verbal post-op instructions are provided — including medications, diet, oral hygiene, and signs to watch for. A 1-week follow-up is scheduled to check healing and remove sutures if needed.

After Your Treatment

Recovery & Aftercare After Wisdom Tooth Removal

Most patients can return to normal activities within 1–2 days. Swelling and mild discomfort peak at 48–72 hours then gradually improve. Full healing takes 7–14 days.

What to Expect Day by Day

  • Day 1: Bite on gauze for 45 minutes, rest, avoid rinsing. Some bleeding and swelling is normal.
  • Day 2–3: Swelling peaks. Apply ice pack for 20 min on/off. Take prescribed medications.
  • Day 4–5: Swelling begins to reduce. Soft diet. Warm salt water rinse from day 2 onwards.
  • Day 6–7: Significantly improved. Follow-up visit at Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi to check healing.

Dry Socket Prevention Tips

  • Do NOT smoke for at least 72 hours after extraction
  • Do NOT use straws — suction dislodges the blood clot
  • Avoid spitting forcefully
  • Eat soft foods — yoghurt, dal, rice, smoothies
  • Take all prescribed medications as directed
  • Contact us immediately if severe pain develops after day 2
After Healing

When to Seek Immediate Help

While complications are rare, contact Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi immediately if you experience:

Dry Socket

Severe, worsening pain 2–3 days after extraction, often with a bad taste or visible empty socket. Treatable quickly — do not delay.

Excessive Bleeding

Heavy bleeding that does not stop with gauze pressure after 1 hour. Bite on a clean folded cloth and call us immediately.

Swelling Getting Worse After Day 3

Progressive swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing after the third day may indicate infection. Call +91 84848 51686 immediately for emergency treatment.
