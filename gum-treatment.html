What Is Gum Disease?

Healthy Gums Are the Foundation of a Healthy Smile

Gum disease (periodontal disease) is a bacterial infection of the tissues surrounding and supporting your teeth. It begins as gingivitis — inflammation and bleeding of the gums — and can progress to periodontitis, where infection destroys the bone and ligaments anchoring your teeth, leading to tooth loss.

The alarming reality: gum disease affects nearly 80% of Indian adults to some degree, yet most people only notice it when it has advanced significantly. Symptoms like bleeding gums are often dismissed as normal — but they are never normal. Healthy gums do not bleed.

At Swaram Dental Clinic, Hinjewadi, Dr. Swati Shivane — MDS postgraduate specialist in Endodontics & Conservative Dentistry — provides comprehensive periodontal care: from professional cleaning for early gingivitis to full deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) for advanced periodontitis. Early treatment is always more effective, less invasive, and less costly.

The systemic connection: Research strongly links gum disease to heart disease, diabetes, respiratory conditions, and pregnancy complications. Treating your gums is not just about your teeth — it is an investment in your overall health.

Warning Signs

Signs You May Have Gum Disease

If you notice any of these symptoms, visit Swaram Dental Clinic in Hinjewadi immediately. Early gum disease is fully reversible — delay makes treatment more complex.

  • Bleeding gums when brushing or flossing — the most common early warning sign
  • Red, swollen, or tender gums — indicates active inflammation
  • Receding gums — teeth appearing longer as gums pull away
  • Persistent bad breath — bacteria in deep gum pockets cause chronic halitosis
  • Loose or shifting teeth — sign of significant bone loss
  • Pus between gums and teeth — sign of abscess or active infection
Risk Factors

What Causes Gum Disease?

🦘

Plaque Build-up

The primary cause. Bacterial plaque not removed by regular brushing and flossing hardens into tartar and infects the gums.

🚬

Smoking & Tobacco

Smokers are 2-7x more likely to develop gum disease and respond less well to treatment. Tobacco masks bleeding, delaying diagnosis.

🥈

Diabetes

Diabetics have a higher risk of gum disease, and gum disease worsens blood sugar control — a two-way relationship.

🧬

Hormonal Changes

Pregnancy, puberty, and menopause can make gums more sensitive to plaque and more susceptible to inflammation.

Understanding Gum Disease

Stages of Gum Disease & Treatment at Each Stage

Gum disease is progressive. Early-stage is fully reversible. Advanced stages require deeper treatment but can still be controlled. The key: do not wait.

Stage 1: Gingivitis

Gums are red, swollen, and bleed easily. No bone loss yet. Fully reversible with professional scaling and improved home care. Treatment: 1-2 visits.

Stage 2: Early Periodontitis

Infection spreads below the gum line. Early bone loss begins. Gum pockets deepen. Treatment: deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) under local anaesthesia.

Stage 3: Moderate Periodontitis

Significant bone and attachment loss. Teeth may begin to feel loose. Treatment: full-mouth scaling and root planing, antibiotic therapy, intensive maintenance.

Stage 4: Advanced Periodontitis

Severe bone loss. Teeth loose, may shift. Risk of tooth loss is high. Treatment: surgical intervention may be required alongside intensive periodontal therapy.

Maintenance Phase

After active treatment, regular 3-6 monthly periodontal maintenance at Swaram Dental Clinic keeps gum disease controlled and prevents relapse.

Prevention

6-monthly professional cleaning, daily brushing & flossing, and early intervention prevent gum disease from progressing to the destructive stages.

Step by Step

How Gum Treatment is Done at Swaram Dental Clinic

Dr. Swati Shivane follows a thorough, evidence-based periodontal protocol to diagnose, treat, and maintain gum health for every patient.

  1. 01

    Periodontal Examination & Probing

    Dr. Swati Shivane measures gum pocket depths around every tooth using a periodontal probe. This maps the severity of disease and guides the treatment plan.

  2. 02

    Digital X-ray — Bone Level Assessment

    Digital radiographs reveal bone loss around the roots, enabling Dr. Shivane to stage the disease accurately and identify teeth at greatest risk.

  3. 03

    Professional Scaling (Supragingival)

    Ultrasonic instruments remove plaque and tartar deposits from above the gum line. Teeth are then polished to remove surface stains and create a clean surface.

  4. 04

    Root Planing — Deep Cleaning (Subgingival)

    For periodontitis, root planing removes bacterial deposits from below the gum line and smooths root surfaces under local anaesthesia. This allows gum tissue to reattach to clean root surfaces.

  5. 05

    Antibiotic Therapy (if required)

    In cases of active infection, local or systemic antibiotics are prescribed to eliminate bacteria that scaling alone cannot reach in deep pockets.

  6. 06

    Review & Re-evaluation

    4-6 weeks after treatment, Dr. Swati Shivane re-probes pocket depths to assess healing, confirms treatment success, and decides if further intervention is needed.

  7. 07

    Periodontal Maintenance Programme

    Regular 3-6 monthly maintenance visits at Swaram Dental Clinic keep gums healthy, prevent relapse, and catch any recurrence early for prompt treatment.

After Gum Treatment

Recovery & Home Care After Gum Treatment

Most patients return to normal activities the same day. Some sensitivity and mild gum soreness for 2-3 days is normal after deep cleaning.

What to Expect

  • Gum soreness and sensitivity for 2-3 days — normal and temporary
  • Teeth may feel more sensitive to cold temporarily as gum inflammation reduces
  • Some minor bleeding may occur for 1-2 days after deep cleaning
  • Gums may appear to recede slightly as swelling resolves — this is healthy tissue returning to normal

Home Care for Healthy Gums

  • Brush gently twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush
  • Floss daily — gum disease begins between teeth where brushes cannot reach
  • Use an antibacterial mouthwash as recommended by Dr. Swati Shivane
  • Quit smoking — tobacco dramatically worsens gum disease and treatment outcomes
  • Keep scheduled maintenance appointments at Swaram Dental Clinic
