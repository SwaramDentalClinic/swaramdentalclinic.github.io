What is Root Canal Treatment?

Save Your Natural Tooth with Expert RCT

Root Canal Treatment (RCT), also called endodontic therapy, is a procedure to remove infected or inflamed pulp from inside a tooth, clean and disinfect the root canals, and seal them with a biocompatible material. Your natural tooth is saved — no extraction needed.

At Swaram Dental Clinic in Hinjewadi, Dr. Swati Shivane — an MDS postgraduate specialist in Endodontics & Conservative Dentistry — performs root canal treatment using the latest rotary endodontic systems, electronic apex locators, and digital RVG radiography. The procedure is completely painless under local anaesthesia and can often be completed in a single sitting.

Modern RCT is no more uncomfortable than getting a filling, and it relieves the severe toothache caused by infection — rather than causing it.

Why specialist care matters: An MDS in Endodontics is a 3-year postgraduate specialisation beyond BDS — specifically focused on root canal anatomy, complex canal systems, and long-term tooth preservation. Do You Need RCT?

Signs You May Need Root Canal Treatment

If you experience any of these symptoms, visit Swaram Dental Clinic in Hinjewadi immediately. Early treatment saves your tooth.

  • Severe, persistent toothache — especially when biting or applying pressure
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers after the stimulus is removed
  • Darkening or discolouration of the affected tooth
  • Swollen, tender gums around the affected tooth
  • Pimple or bump on the gum — sign of abscess or infection
  • Deep cavity that has reached the tooth's nerve
Why Does It Happen?

Common Causes of Pulp Infection

🦷

Deep Tooth Decay

Untreated cavities progress deeper until bacteria reach the pulp chamber and cause infection.

💥

Dental Trauma

Injury to a tooth can damage the pulp even without a visible crack.

🔁

Repeated Dental Work

Multiple fillings or procedures on the same tooth can irritate and eventually inflame the pulp.

🔓

Cracked Tooth

Cracks allow bacteria to reach the pulp, leading to infection and abscess.

Why Choose RCT Over Extraction

Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

Saving your natural tooth is always the best option. Here's why root canal treatment is superior to extraction.

Save Your Natural Tooth

Nothing replaces your own tooth. RCT removes infection while preserving the natural tooth structure for a lifetime of function.

Immediate Pain Relief

RCT eliminates the infected pulp causing severe toothache. Most patients feel significant relief within 24–48 hours.

Cost-Effective vs Extraction

RCT + crown preserves your natural tooth at a fraction of the cost of extraction + dental implant. Extraction may seem cheaper upfront but leads to significantly higher costs for tooth replacement long term.

Prevents Bone Loss

Keeping your natural tooth root in the jaw preserves bone structure and prevents sunken facial appearance.

Stops Infection Spread

An untreated dental abscess can spread to neighbouring teeth. RCT stops the infection at its source.

Restored Normal Function

After RCT and a crown, your tooth functions normally — eat, speak, and smile with confidence.

Step by Step

How Root Canal Treatment is Done at Swaram Dental Clinic

Our standardised, evidence-based protocol ensures a painless, efficient, and successful root canal experience.

  1. 01

    Examination & Digital X-ray

    Dr. Swati Shivane examines the tooth and takes a digital X-ray to assess infection extent, root canal anatomy, and bone involvement.

  2. 02

    Local Anaesthesia

    The area is thoroughly numbed. You feel no pain during the procedure. Topical gel is applied first to make even the injection comfortable.

  3. 03

    Rubber Dam Isolation

    A rubber dam keeps the tooth dry and free of saliva — ensuring a sterile field throughout the procedure.

  4. 04

    Pulp Removal & Canal Shaping

    The infected pulp is removed using precision rotary endodontic files. Root canals are shaped to allow thorough cleaning and sealing.

  5. 05

    Disinfection & Irrigation

    Canals are irrigated with antimicrobial solutions to eliminate all bacteria. Apex locator confirms working length accuracy.

  6. 06

    Canal Filling with Gutta-Percha

    Cleaned canals are filled with gutta-percha — a biocompatible material — and sealed to prevent future bacterial entry.

  7. 07

    Crown Restoration

    A dental crown is recommended to protect the treated tooth from fracture and restore full biting strength and aesthetics.

After Your Treatment

Recovery & Aftercare After RCT

Most patients return to normal activities the same day. Some mild tenderness for 2–3 days is normal and easily managed with over-the-counter pain relief.

What to Expect

  • Mild tenderness when biting for 2–3 days — normal and temporary
  • Some swelling may occur — an ice pack can help
  • Avoid chewing hard food on the treated side until crowned
  • The tooth may feel slightly different for a few days — this settles

Long-Term Care Tips

  • Get a dental crown placed as soon as possible after RCT
  • Brush twice daily and floss regularly
  • Schedule regular dental check-ups every 6 months
  • Report any persistent pain or swelling promptly
